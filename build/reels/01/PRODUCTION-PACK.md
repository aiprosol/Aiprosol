### B · Generate the voiceover at ElevenLabs

- Pick voice: **"Brian"** or **"Adam"** (calm mid-range male, on-brand for Arora-context narration)
- Settings: **Stability 50%, Similarity 75%, Style 30%, Speaker boost ON**
- Paste this script (copy block below) → Generate → Download as `vo.mp3` → drop into `/build/reels/01/`

```
I'm the only human at my company's C-suite table.

Our CEO is named Arora. She's an AI.

She runs strategy, customer interactions, content — most of the day-to-day. I handle the five or ten strategic calls a month that genuinely need a human.

This isn't a thought experiment.

A law firm — 45 hours per partner per week reclaimed. Three-week ROI.

A real estate agency — lead response, six hours to three minutes. Conversion up 28 percent.

A manufacturer — defect rate 4 percent to 0.6 percent.

If we're going to sell automation, our company should run on it. Otherwise we're selling vibes.

Free 60-second ROI Audit. Link in bio.

**Listen back.** If it's stilted, regenerate. If it nails ~35 seconds of speech, you're done.

## 2 · Open CapCut · build the timeline

### Project setup
- New project → **Aspect ratio 9:16** (1080×1920) → frame rate **30fps**

### Track layout (top to bottom on the CapCut timeline)
```
V3 — overlays (text PNGs, transparent — float on top)
V2 — captions (auto-burned from captions.srt)
V1 — video clips (your 10 stock shots, end-to-end)
A1 — voiceover (vo.mp3, full volume)

## 6 · If something breaks

| Problem | Fix |
|---|---|
| Voiceover audio sounds robotic at certain phrases | Regenerate that paragraph alone in ElevenLabs, splice in CapCut |
| The text overlays look small on mobile preview | Scale them up 110-120% in CapCut — the PNGs are 1080×1920 native so they scale cleanly |
| A stock clip is the wrong vibe and you can't find a replacement | Use it anyway. Velocity > perfection on Reel #1. Reels #2-5 benefit from what you learn here. |
| Voiceover + music overpower each other | Lower the music to -22dB. Voice should be ~3× louder than music throughout. |
| Captions show up in CapCut but look bad | Auto-translate from `captions.srt` is reliable. If they look wrong, manually clean them — captions matter (60-85% of viewers are muted). |
| CapCut won't import Space Grotesk | Download from https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Space+Grotesk · install on system · restart CapCut |
