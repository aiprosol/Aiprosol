How to use this document

This is a structured 90-minute walk through your business looking for manual work that an automation can do better. You'll move through 7 functional areas, scoring each on a 1–5 scale. At the end you'll have a prioritized shortlist of automation candidates and an estimate of the hours/£ each one is costing you today.

Materials needed: - 90 minutes uninterrupted - Your last full month's bank/accounting export (look at outflows) - Access to whichever inboxes / Slack channels / shared drives are most active - Two cups of coffee

The rule of thumb: if a process is predictable (same inputs lead to same outputs) and recurring (happens more than once per week), it is an automation candidate. Score every yes-answer, then prioritise in §9.

§1. Lead intake & qualification

# Question Score 1–5
1.1 Do you respond to every inbound enquiry within 5 minutes during business hours?
1.2 Is every form submission automatically created as a CRM record with no human typing?
1.3 Are leads automatically scored by fit (industry, employees, intent) before a human sees them?
1.4 Does the first reply email reference the prospect's specific situation, not a template?
1.5 Are calendar links sent automatically with the first reply?
1.6 Do unanswered leads automatically receive a 2nd, 3rd, 4th touch with rotating angles?
1.7 Do you know your lead-to-meeting conversion rate this week? Last week?

Score interpretation: Each "no" or "score < 3" represents 30–90 min/week of manual work that compound across every lead. If sections 1.1, 1.2, 1.5 are all weak, you are losing 1 in 4 leads to slow response time alone (Harvard Business Review, 2011 — one-hour delay drops contact rate 7×).

§8. The 5-question pressure test

Before you finalise the audit, answer these honestly. If any answer is "yes," there is significant automation upside hiding.

  1. In a typical week, how many times does someone in the company copy data from one system into another by hand? (Each instance = automation candidate.)
  2. What recurring task makes you think "this should be automated already"? (Trust the gut. It's right.)
  3. If you went on holiday for 3 weeks tomorrow, what fires would start? (Each fire = brittle process.)
  4. What's the most boring 30 minutes of your day? (Often the highest-leverage automation target.)
  5. Where do you say "I'll do that this evening / weekend"? (Work that creeps off-hours is work the business won't pay to do — perfect automation candidate.)

§10. What to do with the results

If you scored mostly 4–5s across the board: you're already running a reasonably automated business. Look for the one outlier section and treat it as a polish exercise.

If you have 3–5 sections scoring under 3: you are leaking 20–40 hours/week across the team to manual work. Pick the top 2 candidates from §9 and tackle them first; ROI compounds.

If you scored mostly 1–2s: every direction is upside. Start with §1 (Lead intake) — automating lead response alone routinely returns 3–5× ROI in the first quarter. Then move to §4 (Documents).
