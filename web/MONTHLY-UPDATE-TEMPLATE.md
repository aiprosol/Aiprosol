# Monthly Update Template — Aiprosol

**Owner:** Srijan Paudel
**Use:** Send on the last working day of each month to advisors, supporters, early backers, and the small audience that's expressed serious interest. Standardised format = 30 min to write, not 2 hours.

**Audience definition:** Aiprosol is bootstrapped, no formal investors. "Investor update" here means the 5-50 people who deserve regular substantive updates because they (a) advise informally, (b) referred customers, (c) said "keep me posted" with conviction, or (d) are press contacts who explicitly opted in. Not a marketing newsletter.

**Send infrastructure:** BCC the list from your personal inbox (most personal), or one batch via Resend with a single placeholder image. Don't use Mailchimp/Substack — wrong frame for this audience.

**Send rhythm:** Last working day of every month. Skip months only with explicit notice ("November update will combine into December — taking a quieter month"). Consistency is the signal; never disappear.

## Notes on what to put in / what to leave out

### What goes in

- **The number that genuinely defines the month.** If MRR grew, lead with MRR. If a customer churned, lead with that. If you removed a failed experiment, lead with that.
- **One lowlight per month minimum** (skip only if the month was genuinely clean — most months are not). Honesty about failures is the single highest-trust signal in this format.
- **Three asks max.** More than three and the reader picks zero.
- **One question for the reader where possible.** "What would change your mind about X?" or "Anyone you know who would have a strong opinion on Y?" — opt-in conversation, not lecturing.

### What stays out

- ❌ Marketing copy ("excited to share", "thrilled to announce")
- ❌ Self-congratulation that isn't backed by numbers
- ❌ Pivots framed as wins ("we've decided to refocus on..." — be honest about why)
- ❌ Vague "things are going well" updates — better to send nothing than to send vague
- ❌ Customer names without explicit permission
- ❌ Internal team gossip / drama / personal frustrations
- ❌ Long-form thinkpieces — keep this format tight; publish thinkpieces on Substack / blog
- ❌ Funding talk if you're not raising — invites pressure you don't want

### Frequency rules

- One update per month, every month, for 24+ months. Consistency creates the asset; missing a month resets the trust counter.
- If a defining thing happens mid-month (customer #1 lands, press hit, etc.), send a one-line "between updates" note from your personal email — don't break the monthly cadence with a special-edition update.
- If you genuinely have nothing to report, send anyway with that as the headline: "Quiet month. Two thoughts in progress, both unfinished. Will report in 30 days."

### List management

- Add by mutual agreement only. "Keep me posted" is permission to add; ambiguous interest is not.
- Annual list audit: every December, ask "still want these?" with a one-click unsubscribe.
- Keep the list private. Never tell anyone who else is on it.

## First-month variant (template for Month 1 — the introduction post)

If you've never sent one before, the first one needs framing:

```
Subject: Aiprosol — Month 1 update ({Month Year})

Hi [name],

I'm starting a monthly Aiprosol update. You're on this list because [pick: "you backed me", "you advised on a specific decision", "you said 'keep me posted'", "you're someone I want to keep posted whether you asked or not"]. If that's wrong, just reply "drop me" and I will. Either way, no offence taken.

The format: end of every month, four sections — Numbers, Wins, Lowlights, Asks. Plus three concrete things I'm shipping next month so you can hold me to them.

Month 1 starts now. {Then proceed with the standard template above.}
```
