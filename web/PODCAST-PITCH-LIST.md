# Podcast Pitch List — Aiprosol / Srijan Paudel

**Owner:** Srijan Paudel
**Updated:** 2026-05-21

A ranked shortlist of 18 podcasts where Srijan has a real shot at being booked + a per-show pitch angle. Sequenced from highest fit to lowest. Skip any show where the angle doesn't authentically apply.

**Why this matters:** Podcasts are tier-2 secondary sources for Wikipedia. They also feed referral signal into Search Console + serve as evidence in Forbes 30 Under 30 nominations. Aim for **2-3 placements in the first 6 months** — that's enough to unlock Wikipedia, not so many that they cannibalise each other.

**Pitch hygiene rules:**

1. **One pitch per show.** Tailored. Don't blast a generic email to 20 hosts.
2. **Subject line ≤60 chars.** Hosts triage hundreds of pitches; the subject is everything.
3. **3 sentences max in the email body.** Show + sentence about why your story fits + a link to the manifesto. Nothing else.
4. **Include the audit link.** Booking decisions are made in 30 seconds; the host should be able to verify the company is real in one click. Link to `aiprosol.com/agents` (the live dashboard) — that's the strongest single proof point.
5. **Don't ask for an intro through a mutual connection unless you actually know one well.** Cold pitching the booker/producer email directly is usually faster.

## Tier 1 — Highest fit, pitch first

### 1. Indie Hackers Podcast (Courtland Allen)

- **Audience:** ~150k bootstrapped founders, indie builders
- **Why us:** Single founder, bootstrapped, weird operating model, public dashboard. Pure indie-hacker bait.
- **Angle:** "I run a consultancy with one human (me) and ten AI agents — here's the operating loop in detail"
- **Contact:** [courtland@indiehackers.com](mailto:courtland@indiehackers.com) or DM via the Indie Hackers community
- **Lead time:** Courtland often books 4-6 weeks out
- **Prep before pitching:** Have at least 5 paying customers OR a Show HN front-page run. He prefers founders with traction, not pre-launch.

### 2. Latent Space (swyx + Alessio)

- **Audience:** ~80k AI engineers, infrastructure builders
- **Why us:** They cover production agentic systems heavily; ours is a real live one
- **Angle:** "Production agentic operations — architecture deep-dive: idempotency keys, structured output schemas, audit logging, what we ship vs what we removed"
- **Contact:** [swyx via X](https://x.com/swyx) or via [Smol.ai community](https://www.smol.ai)
- **Lead time:** 4-8 weeks
- **Prep before pitching:** Have a clean architecture diagram + a specific technical anti-pattern story (Latent Space loves "we tried X and it failed, here's why")

### 3. Cognitive Revolution (Nathan Labenz)

- **Audience:** ~50k AI builders + thinkers
- **Why us:** Nathan booked Devin / Cursor / Glean folks — agentic ops fits his lane
- **Angle:** "An AI CEO in production — what the role actually requires and what it can't do"
- **Contact:** [Nathan Labenz on X](https://x.com/labenz) or via [cognitiverevolution.ai](https://www.cognitiverevolution.ai)
- **Lead time:** 6-10 weeks (very high inbound rate)
- **Prep before pitching:** Be ready for technical-philosophical questions. He'll probe the "what's left for the human?" question hard.

### 4. The Knowledge Project (Shane Parrish)

- **Audience:** ~500k generalist (CEOs, investors, thoughtful operators)
- **Why us:** Shane covers mental-model-shifting stories; running a company at 22 with AI agents is one
- **Angle:** "Decision making when your operational team is mostly AI — what does the human do?"
- **Contact:** Via [fs.blog](https://fs.blog/contact) or [@ShaneAParrish on X](https://x.com/ShaneAParrish)
- **Lead time:** 3-6 months (very competitive)
- **Prep before pitching:** Have a thoughtful 1-pager on "what only the human does" — this is the heart of the show

### 5. Practical AI (Daniel Whitenack + Chris Benson)

- **Audience:** ~40k AI practitioners (more enterprise / data science leaning)
- **Why us:** They prioritise production deployments over speculation
- **Angle:** "Running an agentic operating model in production — the patterns that survived vs the ones we removed"
- **Contact:** [practicalai.fm](https://practicalai.fm) — submit guest pitch via their form
- **Lead time:** 4-6 weeks

## After-booking checklist

When a host accepts:

1. **Confirm date + time + recording platform** (Riverside, Zoom, etc.)
2. **Send pre-call doc** with 3-5 "ask me about" topics + 3 things to NOT ask (e.g. don't ask financials by name; do ask about agent failures by name)
3. **Test mic, camera, lighting** the day before
4. **Prep one specific failure story** (most hosts ask "what didn't work?" — have the answer ready in 90 seconds)
5. **Have the URL ready to share live** (aiprosol.com/agents — the live dashboard is the strongest single proof)
6. **Send thank-you after recording** — short, with the dashboard URL again
7. **Promote the episode** when it drops — pinned tweet on @srijanpaudel6, LinkedIn post, Substack mention
8. **Update WIKIPEDIA-DRAFT.md** with the podcast as a ref under the source-acquisition tracker once it's live
