## 4. Reply templates — 5 patterns for industry engagement

These aren't scripts to copy verbatim. They're **structures** to use when high-profile AI/automation people post things you can usefully respond to. The goal is to be the first useful comment, not to spam.

### Pattern 1 — "Receipts, not vibes"

When someone posts a high-level take about AI agents / AI automation that you can ground with specific data:

> [Specific number or observation from your operating data]. We see this in our agent log — [contextual detail].
> 
> [Link to /transparency or specific blog post] if you want to look at the raw runs.

**Example trigger:** Karpathy posts about model reliability. You reply with the actual fallback rate from your agents, link the transparency dashboard.

### Pattern 2 — "What didn't work"

When someone posts an "AI just unlocked X" enthusiasm tweet, the contrarian-but-friendly counter:

> Tried this. Removed it after [time period]. The failure mode was [specific concrete thing]. 
>
> What worked instead: [the actual production pattern].

**Example trigger:** Someone posts about AI replacing customer support. You reply with the auto-send hallucination story from week 2.

### Pattern 3 — "Operator angle"

When someone posts an academic / research-y AI take, ground it with operator reality:

> Operator perspective: [the practical constraint that the academic angle missed]. We hit this when [specific Aiprosol context].
>
> [Link or specific detail].

**Example trigger:** Research paper on agent autonomy. You reply about idempotency keys and the human gate making "autonomous" a misnomer.

### Pattern 4 — "Build-in-public receipt"

When someone in your space ships something, congratulate + share a related receipt from your own ops:

> Congrats [@person]. We shipped something adjacent on the [topic] side last week — [specific thing]. Took [time] and the breakthrough was [counterintuitive insight].
>
> [Link to your relevant post if any].

**Example trigger:** Harrison Chase ships a new LangChain feature. You reply with how you avoid framework lock-in via n8n self-hosted + direct API calls.

### Pattern 5 — "Direct answer"

When someone asks an open question to their feed and you have a specific answer:

> [Direct answer in 1-2 sentences].
>
> Longer version: [link to relevant Aiprosol content].

**Example trigger:** "What's the cost-per-call breakdown for a frontier vs a budget LLM at scale?" — you have actual numbers in the State of AI Automation 2026 post.

### Operating note

Pattern 1 (receipts) is the highest-leverage. The transparency dashboard makes you uniquely positioned to ground vibes with specifics. Use it more than the others.

Pattern 4 (build-in-public receipts) is the second-highest because it builds the relationship with adjacent founders. Don't let it become a stalking pattern — once a week per person max.

Patterns 2, 3, 5 are situational.
